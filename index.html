About me

My passion for quantitative problem solving started at the University of Technology of Troyes where I was fascinated to discover that mathematics and ant colony's pheromone-based communication together, gave birth to ACO, one of the most effective algorithms in computer science. I then graduated with a Master of Engineering in Industrial Engineering, where I had dedicated myself to Operations Research and was fortunate enough to be mentored by some of the greatest minds in this field.

I am now studying towards a Master of Science in Management and Business Analytics at Ivey Business School, the second largest producer of business cases in the world, just behind Harvard. The case-based learning method is an amazing experience that has expanded my knowledge in business key areas and unique characteristics of various industries.

Hailing from France, I am a native French speaker, employing my technical and soft skills at leading companies like Mars and Danone on operations research and data analytics. I now work in Toronto, Canada for SAS Institute as a data scientist.

Having experiences ranging from France to Canada, I have always stayed consistent in loving to learn, whether that be about data science or about the secrets of healthy living. I found myself very interested in reading online articles about artificial intelligence and emerging technologies as well as books related to self-help, psychology and communication.
